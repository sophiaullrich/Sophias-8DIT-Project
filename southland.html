About

The Southland district is an administrative district in New Zealand. It covers the southern tip of the South Island, and is bordered by the Otago and Canterbury regions. The district has a population of around 31,000 people. The district is known for its picturesque landscapes, including the Southern Alps, fiords, lakes and rivers. It is also home to a number of towns and cities, including Invercargill, the regional capital. The Southland district has a long history of agriculture, and today it is known for its dairy farming and sheep farming. The district is also a popular tourist destination, with attractions such as Fiordland National Park, Queenstown and Milford Sound.
