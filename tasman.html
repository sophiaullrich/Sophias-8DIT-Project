About

The Tasman district is a local government district in the north of the South Island of New Zealand. Its main towns are Motueka, Richmond, and Takaka. It covers an area of 8,441 square kilometers (3,259 square miles), and its population is 41,600 (June 2020). The district was established in 1989 when the Nelson City and Tasman County councils amalgamated. The name Tasman was chosen as it was the name of the old county, and also because of the district's location on the Tasman Sea. The district is bounded to the west by the Buller District and the Grey District, to the north by the Golden Bay District, and to the east and south by the Marlborough District.
