About

The North Island of New Zealand is an island in the southwestern Pacific Ocean. It is the second-largest island in New Zealand after the South Island and lies to the northeast of the smaller Stewart Island. The North Island covers a land area of 113,729 square kilometers (43,911 sq mi), making it slightly larger than the British Isles. It is about one-sixth the size of the South Island. The North Island is divided into two main landmasses: the smaller volcanic North Island and the much larger continental North Island.

Pricing

The North Island of New Zealand is home to some of the most expensive real estate in the world. Properties in Auckland, the capital city, routinely sell for millions of dollars. Even small towns and rural areas are not immune to high prices, with homes often going for hundreds of thousands of dollars. There are a number of factors that contribute to the high cost of housing in New Zealand. The country has a limited supply of land, and much of the land that is available is difficult to build on due to the country's topography. In addition, the country's economy is doing well, and this has led to increased demand for housing.

Why Choose the North Island?

There are many reasons to choose the North Island of New Zealand as a place to live. The North Island offers a wide range of scenery and activities, from the beaches of the Bay of Islands to the snow-capped mountains of Tongariro National Park. There are also many great cities and towns to live in, such as Auckland, Wellington and Tauranga. The North Island is also a great place to raise a family. There are excellent schools and plenty of childcare options, as well as plenty of things to do for kids of all ages. The North Island is also a great place to retire to, with plenty of options for retirement living and plenty of things to do to keep active.
