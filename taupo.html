About

The Taupo district is a local government district in the Waikato Region of New Zealand. Its northern boundary lies on the shores of Lake Taupo, New Zealand's largest lake, while to the south it extends to the Tongariro National Park. The district seat is the town of Taupo. The district was established as the Taupo Riding of the Auckland Provincial District on 1 January 1876. It became the Taupo Scenic Reserve District on 1 April 1904 and the Taupo County on 1 March 1906. On 1 April 1974, Taupo County was abolished and absorbed into the Waitomo District and the Hauraki District. The Taupo District was established on 1 November 1989 by the Local Government (Taupo District) Act 1989. The district is governed by the Taupo District Council, a unitary authority that also performs the functions of a regional council.
