About

The South Wairarapa district is located in the southern part of the North Island of New Zealand. It covers an area of 1,700 square kilometres and has a population of about 31,000 people. The district includes the towns of Masterton, Greytown, Featherston and Martinborough. The South Wairarapa district is a predominantly rural area. Agriculture is the main economic activity, with sheep and beef farming being the main types of agriculture. The district also has a growing wine industry. The climate in the South Wairarapa district is mild and temperate. The average annual rainfall is about 1,200 millimetres.

Pricing

Generally, properties closer to the coast are more expensive than those further inland. This is due to the increased demand for coastal properties, as they are often seen as being more desirable. Similarly, larger properties are usually more expensive than smaller properties, as there is more land to purchase. Older properties can also be more expensive than newer properties, as they may be more unique or have more history. Finally, properties in better condition are often more expensive than those in poorer condition, as they will require less work to bring them up to a desirable standard.
